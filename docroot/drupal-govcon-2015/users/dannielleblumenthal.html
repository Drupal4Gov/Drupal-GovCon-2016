DannielleBlumenthal

Dannielle
Blumenthal
Advanced Manufacturing National Program Office

Dr. Dannielle Blumenthal is a communications pioneer. She began her career in branding at Young & Rubicam's Brand Futures Group/Intelligence Factory, and launched pioneering social media programs externally and internally at The Brand Consultancy, U.S. Customs and Border Protection, and USAID. She currently serves as the communications director of the Advanced Manufacturing Program at the National Institute of Science and Technology.

A frequently sought-after speaker and frequently published author and lecturer, Dr. Blumenthal is a regular contributor to GovExec.com on the topics of leadership and communication. She has received numerous industry honors and awards: Klout Top 20% social media producer (2014); SlideShare Top 5% (2013); Meritorious Group Honor Award for website revitalization at USAID (2013); Featured Blog of the Month, USAID (2012); Top 1% most viewed LinkedIn profiles (2012); #2 Most Influential GovLoop.com member (2012); Commissioner's Award, U.S. Customs and Border Protection (2011); IABC-DC Silver Inkwell Award (2008); & more. 

Dr. Blumenthal is a frequent participant in DigitalGov's Social Media Community of Practice and its Open Opportunities Program (2014-present), chaired the Federal Communicators Network (2013), co-launched the Federal Social Media Subcouncil (2009); served on the board of the Journal of Brand Management (2003), and revitalized the global Institute for Brand Leadership (2001-2003).

She holds a B.A. in interdisciplinary studies/creative writing (SUNY Empire State College, 1991); a Ph.D. in sociology (CUNY Graduate Center, 1997); and Graduate Certification in Organization Development (Fielding University, 20015). 

Follow her @oursocialfuture.
